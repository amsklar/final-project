Educator

Alyssa is very passionate about teaching GIS, Programming, and Statistics in the context of Geography, Environmental Sciences, and Urban Planning. She believes that knowledge is not solely developed in a classroom, that inclusive teaching must disrupt power dynamics, we must center anti-racist and decolonizing pedagogy, and that critical thinking is the biggest takeaway from all learning.

Alyssa actively seeks to learn alternative strategies to teaching and assessment in higher education, especially related to technology.

Engagement, Career Training, and Outreach (ECO) Program Award Recipient

Funded project to develop user-friendly GIS curriculum for the University of Michigan's Earth Camp, the Earth and Environmental Sciences high-school outreach program for college-bound underrepresented minority students in the Environmental Sciences. Modules focus on the basics of Esri ArcGIS through mapping exercises exploring Great Lakes environmental issues being studied by CIGLR with a focus on environmental justice.

Funding for this project was provided by the University of Michigan Cooperative Institute for Great Lakes Research (CIGLR), through the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) Cooperative Agreement NA22OAR4320150. This is CIGLR contribution $7,982.

Graduate Teaching Certificate (GTC)
